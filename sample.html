BUCK Impact Report 2023

2024 marks BUCK’s 20th anniversary, and I’m thinking about how far we’ve come. When Ryan, Jeff, and I started this company from a Craigslist ad, we had one goal: make great work while taking good care of our people. Anyone who’s tried this knows it’s anything but simple — it requires daily dedication, accountability, and a commitment to continual improvement. This impact report showcases our strides and affirms that dedication to our vision. I am immensely proud of what we’ve achieved and excited for the future we are building together at BUCK.

Orion Tait, Cofounder of BUCK

  1. 2004

    BUCK begins as a motion graphics studio after a chance meeting on Craigslist in 2004. From day one, we work alongside gifted collaborators, investing in our people and our creative above all.

  2. 2006

    We move from our starter space into our first purpose-built office in Los Angeles, CA.

    We open BUCK’s New York office.

  3. 2008

    Our first attempt at mixing miniatures with 3D in a series of spots for Toyota.

  4. 2010

    We create what we consider to be the first online explainer video for the documentary Waiting for Superman.

  5. 2012

    Launch of our self-funded project Good Books, winning over 15 awards. This is widely seen as a breakthrough moment for BUCK.

  6. 2014

    Deliver Intentions for Apple.

  7. 2015

    BUCK Sydney opens.

  8. 2017

    First ever Emmy win for the opening sequence of David Blaine’s Spectacle of the Real.

  9. 2018

    BUCK LA moves to Lacy Street campus.

    Launch of our first AR app, Slapstick.

2 0 2 0

BUCK adapts to remote work and against all odds, adds 111 staffers and hundreds of freelancers to our global roster.

The social movements of 2020 catalyze an understanding of BUCK’s duty to our communities, industry, and Earth. We begin actively pursuing impact projects that foster creative partnerships in social justice, sustainability, and arts education.

  1. 2021

    BUCK Amsterdam opens.

2022

We introduce Residence, a network pioneered by BUCK to champion best-in-class work while empowering the community that makes it possible.

Residence collaborations are an opportunity to grow our capabilities through a trusted and vetted network of excellence.

Introducing Giant Ant & Anyways into the BUCK ecosystem

We brought Giant Ant and Anyways even closer to BUCK, providing their teams with backend support through business intelligence, HR, and finance. We hope to continue working closely and sharing resources, relationships, and opportunities to learn from each other while pushing the boundaries of what we can achieve together.
